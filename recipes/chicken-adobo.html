Chicken Adobo

Savory, tangy Filipino dish with chicken, soy sauce, and vinegar

Chicken adobo is a beloved Filipino dish with a rich and aromatic flavor profile. The dish is typically made by marinating chicken in a combination of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices, then slowly simmered until tender. The result is a comforting, savory meal with a balance of salty, tangy, and slightly sweet notes.

Adobo is often served with rice and can vary slightly across regions, with some recipes adding coconut milk, potatoes, or potatoes to the dish. This stew-like dish holds cultural significance in the Philippines, where it is considered the national dish and is frequently enjoyed as a family meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the marinade along with the water, then bring to a simmer.
  4. Cover and cook for 30-40 minutes, until the chicken is tender.
  5. Optionally, add sugar for a slight sweetness and stir.
  6. Serve hot with rice. Enjoy!
