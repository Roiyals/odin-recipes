Carbonara

Creamy pasta with eggs and pancetta

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with a creamy sauce of eggs, cheese, pancetta or bacon, and black pepper. The sauce is made by tossing the hot pasta with the egg mixture, allowing the heat to create a silky, smooth coating. The dish is rich and flavorful, with the crispy pancetta adding texture and the cheese providing a savory, tangy depth.

It's traditionally served with spaghetti, but other pasta types can be used as well. Carbonara is beloved for its simple ingredients and comforting, indulgent taste.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (if using garlic)

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat and cook pancetta or bacon until crispy. If using garlic, add it to the pan and cook for 1 minute.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan, Pecorino, and a pinch of black pepper.
  4. Once pasta is cooked, toss it with the pancetta (off the heat), then add the egg mixture, stirring quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to reach desired consistency.
  5. Serve immediately, topped with extra cheese and black pepper. Enjoy!
