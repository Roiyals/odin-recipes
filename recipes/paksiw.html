Paksiw

Filipino fish stew

Paksiw is a traditional Filipino dish where meat, often pork or fish, is simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and various spices. The dish is characterized by its tangy, savory, and slightly sweet flavor, with the vinegar acting as a tenderizer and adding depth to the taste.

Paksiw is typically served with rice and can include ingredients like vegetables or banana blossoms, depending on the variation. It's a comforting, flavorful dish that is popular in Filipino home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) pork belly or fish (bangus or tilapia), cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a pot, combine the pork (or fish), soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, and water. Let it marinate for 15-30 minutes.
  2. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer.
  3. Cook for about 30-40 minutes (for pork) or 15-20 minutes (for fish), until tender and the flavors meld.
  4. Optionally, add sugar to balance the tangy flavor and stir well.
  5. Taste and adjust with salt if needed.
  6. Serve with steamed rice and enjoy!
